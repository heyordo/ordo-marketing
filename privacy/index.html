Privacy Policy

Ordo · Last updated: [LAUNCH DATE]

Short version: Ordo stores your dumps on your own device. The text you capture is sent to our AI partner solely to sort it, and is not retained beyond that processing. We have no accounts, no tracking, no ads, and no analytics inside the app. If the app crashes, an anonymous technical report helps us fix it. Our website uses cookie-less, aggregate-only visitor counts. You can delete everything anytime from the app.

1. Who we are

Ordo (the "App") is published by Samuel Laredo ("we", "us", "our"). You can reach us at support@heyordo.app.

2. What we collect

We collect the minimum needed to make Ordo work:

  • The text you capture. When you dump a thought into Ordo, that text is sent to our AI partner to be sorted and refined (see Section 3). We do not store this text on any server we control — your dumps live on your device.
  • App preferences. Things like dark/light mode, your reminders, and whether you've completed the intro. Stored locally on your device only.
  • Anonymous crash reports. If the app crashes or hits an error, a technical report is sent to Sentry (Functional Software, Inc.), our crash-reporting service. These reports contain technical information (device model, OS version, the error and where in the code it happened) but no personal data and none of your dump content. Apple may additionally share its own crash reports with us if you've opted in to that on your device.
  • Website visits (heyordo.app only, not the App). Our website uses Plausible Analytics, a privacy-first service that counts visits in aggregate — no cookies, no personal data, no advertising identifiers, no cross-site tracking. This runs only on the website; the App itself contains no analytics.

We do not collect:

  • Your name, email, phone, or any account information (we have no accounts).
  • Your location.
  • Your contacts, photos, or other device data.
  • Advertising identifiers or device fingerprints.
  • In-app usage analytics or behavioral tracking of any kind.

3. AI processing — how the sorting works

When you save a dump, Ordo sends the text of that dump to Anthropic, PBC ("Anthropic"), the maker of the Claude AI model. Anthropic processes the text and returns a sorted/cleaned version, which Ordo then displays.

What this means in practice:

  • Each dump's text leaves your device once, for the purpose of sorting and auto-correcting it.
  • Anthropic processes the text under their own privacy terms, available at anthropic.com/legal/privacy. As of the date above, Anthropic states that API inputs from commercial customers are not used to train their models.
  • We do not retain the text on our own servers after processing.
  • We never send your dumps to anyone else, sell them, or use them for any purpose other than sorting them and returning them to you.

If you do not want your dumps processed by AI, you can use Ordo in offline mode (the in-app sort will fall back to a basic on-device rules-based sort, with no AI involvement).

4. Where your data lives

Everything you create in Ordo — your dumps, reminders, completed items, settings — is stored on your device using iOS's local storage. Nothing syncs to any cloud we control. If you delete the app, that data is gone from your device.

You can export a full backup of your data anytime from Settings → Data & Backup → Export. You can also restore a backup the same way.

5. Your rights and choices

  • Delete your data. Open the app and use Settings → Data & Backup → Clear all, or simply delete the app from your device.
  • Export your data. Settings → Data & Backup → Export downloads everything as a JSON file you control.
  • Opt out of AI sorting. Turn off internet access for Ordo in iOS Settings. Ordo will fall back to local sorting only.
  • Reach us. Email support@heyordo.app with any questions about your data or this policy.

Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights under laws like GDPR (EU/UK) or CCPA (California), including the right to access, correct, or delete personal data. Because we don't collect personal data tied to you, most of these rights are satisfied by the controls above. If you have a specific request, email us.

6. Children

Ordo is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If you believe a child has used Ordo, contact us and we'll help.

7. Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, we'll change the date at the top and, for material changes, notify you in the app on next launch. Your continued use after a change means you accept the updated policy.

8. Contact

Questions, feedback, or requests about privacy: support@heyordo.app.
